But one thing within your grasp is sunscreen. Your sunscreen could contaminate large bodies of water and harm reefs. Many coastal countries are starting to ban oxybenzone and microplastic sunscreens due to their highly toxic chemicals. A 2015 study shows that ONE DROP of oxybenzone sunscreen can contaminate 6.5 Olympic-sized pools (Archives of Environmental Contamination and Toxicology). With only about 25% of the reefs in the Great Barrier Reef off the coastline of Australia still alive compared to the year 2000, the smallest actions matter (Great Barrier Reef Foundation Analysis). Not only are corals in danger, with many non-mineral sunscreens including microplastics, you could be suffocating a sea turtle, or even contributing to a whale's demise. By getting into water with sunscreen, your sunscreen dissolves into the ocean. Instead of spreading the harm, you should spread the word and find the right brands at your local store to save the ocean.
